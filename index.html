Sala Silvergruva

- The mine began to be mined at the end of the 15th century and was worked until 1908 when it closed. Mining resumed after the Second World War and continued until 1962. - Storgruvan consists of approx. 20 km of areas (mining tunnels), approx. 200 rock rooms and countless shafts. - In winter, there are a couple of thousand bats in the mine. - At the end of the 17th century, Queen Christina's shaft was the world's deepest and is still the world's largest man-made mine shaft. Carl XI's shaft was the mine's deepest with a depth of 318.6 meters, i.e. almost as deep as the height of the Eiffel Tower!
